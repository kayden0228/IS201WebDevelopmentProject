About My Travels

Growing up in Castle Rock, Colorado, I've always been drawn to adventure — whether that's hiking in the Rockies, swimming in mountain lakes, or traveling internationally for meaningful service and exploration. Travel has taught me more about the world, other cultures, and myself than almost anything else.

The most defining chapter of my life was a 2-year service mission to the Marshall Islands for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, where I lived among the Marshallese people, learned their language, and dedicated myself to sharing the gospel of Jesus Christ and helping individuals and families grow closer to Him.

I've also traveled to Kenya to document the opening of the country's first public children's cancer hospital, sailed around the U.S. Virgin Islands with my family, explored the beaches and ports of the Bahamas, and relaxed on the Pacific coast of Punta Mita, Mexico. Every destination has left its mark.

Marshall Islands: Two Years of Service

Of everything I've experienced in my life, nothing compares to the two years I spent living in the Marshall Islands as a full-time missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. This wasn't a vacation — it was a complete immersion in a culture, a language, and a people that I came to deeply love.

I learned to speak Marshallese, one of the most unique and complex languages in the Pacific, so that I could genuinely connect with the people I was there to serve. Day after day, I knocked on doors, sat with families, and shared my testimony of Jesus Christ — teaching about His gospel and how coming closer to Him can transform lives.

The Marshall Islands are a breathtakingly beautiful chain of low-lying atolls in the central Pacific, with turquoise lagoons, white sand beaches, and some of the most genuine and warm communities I've ever encountered. The experience shaped who I am and deepened my faith, my compassion, and my understanding of what truly matters.

Kenya: A Life-Changing Mission

In one of the most meaningful experiences of my life, I traveled to Kenya for two weeks as part of a humanitarian mission. Our team was there to film and document the opening of Kenya's first public children's cancer hospital — an extraordinary milestone for the country and a beacon of hope for countless families.

The experience gave me a firsthand look at the challenges facing healthcare in developing nations, while also showing me the incredible strength of community, compassion, and human resilience. I was responsible for capturing the event on camera, conducting interviews with staff and families, and producing documentary-style content.

It was a humbling reminder of how much of a difference one institution — one act of service — can make in the lives of thousands.
